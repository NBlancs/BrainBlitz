BrainBlitz Design System
This guide outlines the visual language to help you implement the retro-minimalist style across your app.

1. Color Palette
Background: #FFFFFF (Pure White)
Primary Accent (Buttons/Title): #0055FF (Vibrant Blue)
Borders/Text: #000000 (Pure Black)
Secondary Accents (Answers/Categories):
Success: #4CAF50 (Green)
Warning: #FFC107 (Yellow)
Error/Action: #F44336 (Red)
Neutral/Shadow: #E0E0E0 (Light Grey for subtle shadows or pressed effects)
2. Typography
Title/Headers: Use a pixel-art font (e.g., Press Start 2P or VT323).
Usage: App Logo, "MISSION COMPLETE", "SELECT MISSION".
Body/Instructions: Use a clean, modern Mono-spaced font (e.g., Roboto Mono or JetBrains Mono).
Usage: Questions, User handles, instructions.
3. Component Styling
Input Fields: 3px - 5px solid black borders. No rounded corners (square) or very slight rounding (max 4px). Include a "block" style cursor that blinks.
Buttons:
"Arcade" Style: Solid color background with a 4px black bottom shadow to create a 3D "pressable" look.
Interaction: When pressed, the button should shift down 4px and the shadow should disappear.
Cards: Thick black outlines with 8-bit style icons. Keep internal padding generous to maintain the "minimalist" feel.
Progress/Health Bars: Use a series of pixelated hearts (❤️) or solid squares (■) rather than a smooth continuous bar.
4. Iconography
Style: 8-bit / Pixel Art.
Navigation: Use simple, chunky pixelated icons for Home, Leaderboard, Profile, and Settings.
Scale: Ensure icons are crisp; avoid anti-aliasing to keep that sharp "retro" edge.
5. Layout Principles
White Space: Maintain significant white space around elements. This is the key to balancing "Retro" with "Minimalism."
Grid: Align elements to a strict grid to reinforce the structured, digital feel of old-school games.
